Research

I'm a PhD student in the Human Rights, Big Data and Human Rights Project at the University of Essex. I'm researching the ways in which gender stereotypes are encoded into automated decision-making systems, particularly in the UK public sector. My supervisor is Professor Lorna McGregor.

I'm also occasionally a human rights gender consultant and advisor, with specific expertise in discrimination on the grounds of sexual orientation, gender identity and sex characteristics. Email me for more information.

Work history

From 2011 to 2019, I worked at the International Secretariat of Amnesty International, of which six years were spent in research and policy advice roles.

Prior to this, I've worked as a caseworker for domestic violence and hate crime cases, a freelance maths tutor, a bartender, and an assistant language teacher for elementary and junior high school students in rural Wakayama, Japan.

I have an MA in Gender Studies from SOAS and a BA in Mathematics from the University of Cambridge.

Board and advisory work

I'm a trustee of the Feminist Review Trust, which allocates c.GBP25,000 to feminist projects worldwide annually.

Side projects
